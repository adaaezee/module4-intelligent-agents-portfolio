Professional Skills Matrix and Action Plan (PDP)

The following matrix documents the professional skills developed throughout the Intelligent Agents module, providing evidence of learning and an action plan for continued development.

Table 1: Professional Skills Matrix

Skill Evidence from Intelligent Agents Module Reflection and Development Action Plan
Time Management Balancing full-time work, international events and medical appointments alongside module deadlines and group project contributions. Engagement was inconsistent towards the later units due to competing demands. Improved prioritisation is needed. Communicate module deadlines proactively to my employer and block time in advance for seminars and submissions.
Commercial Awareness Designing a BDI multi-agent system for a fictional client (Insight Analytics Ltd) with real organisational requirements; applying generative AI knowledge to my marketing role. Developed understanding of how intelligent agent technologies can address real business problems and create operational efficiencies. Continue exploring AI adoption trends in marketing and technology sectors to stay commercially informed.
Critical Thinking and Analysis Evaluating trade-offs between reactive and deliberative architectures in the group project; analysing ethical implications of generative AI across three discussion forums. Strengthened ability to assess competing approaches and present evidence-based arguments. Tutor feedback highlighted opportunity to deepen comparative analysis. In future projects, dedicate more time to comparing alternative approaches before settling on a design decision.
Communication and Literacy Written contributions to three collaborative discussion forums; peer responses; coordinating team communications; circulating the final report for sign-off. Improved ability to communicate complex technical concepts clearly in academic writing and collaborative contexts. Continue developing academic writing skills, particularly in synthesising literature to support critical arguments.
IT and Digital Designing UML diagrams in draw.io; writing and testing Python code for the BDI pipeline; building and maintaining an HTML e-portfolio in VS Code; publishing via GitHub Pages. Developed practical confidence across multiple technical tools, directly transferable to a technical career in AI. Build on Python skills developed in this module and explore additional agent frameworks such as AutoGen or LangChain.
Resilience Completing the module while managing full-time work, international events and medical appointments across the final months. Demonstrated ability to maintain academic commitment under significant personal and professional pressure. Develop a more structured self-care and workload management strategy to sustain performance across future modules.
Interpersonal Collaborating with Group E across multiple weeks; building positive working relationships with teammates despite having no prior connection. Overcame apprehension about group work and contributed meaningfully to a high-performing team. Seek out collaborative opportunities in future modules and professional settings to continue building confidence in team environments.
Problem-Solving Iterating on the ProcessingAgent activity diagram to incorporate correct loop logic; structuring the BDI architecture to meet client requirements; debugging Python code. Learned to approach technical design problems methodically, refining outputs based on review and testing. Apply systematic troubleshooting approaches to future technical projects, documenting design decisions as I go.
Ethical Awareness Researching ethical implications of DALL-E and ChatGPT in Discussion 3; evaluating bias, misinformation and the EU AI Act; reflecting on deepfake regulation. Broadened professional awareness of ethical responsibilities tied to AI technologies, particularly relevant to my marketing role. Actively follow developments in AI regulation (EU AI Act, UK AI Safety Institute) and apply ethical considerations in professional use of generative AI tools.
Teamwork, Global Citizen and Leadership Sending the team contract; coordinating final report submission on behalf of the group; assigning tasks based on individual strengths. Took on a coordinating and leadership role within the team naturally, ensuring the project was submitted to standard. Develop formal project management skills to complement natural leadership tendencies in future collaborative work.
Critical Reflection Structured reflection using Rolfe et al.'s (2001) What / So What / Now What framework; critically engaging with tutor feedback on the group project. Developed ability to reflect honestly on both achievements and limitations, and to identify concrete actions for improvement. Continue using a structured reflective framework across all remaining modules as part of ongoing professional development.

References

  • Rolfe, G., Freshwater, D. and Jasper, M. (2001) Critical Reflection in Nursing and the Helping Professions: A User's Guide. Basingstoke: Palgrave Macmillan.
