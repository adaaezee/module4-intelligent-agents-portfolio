Summary of Learning Outcomes

This page maps the activities and artefacts produced throughout the Intelligent Agents module to the four module learning outcomes, demonstrating how each piece of work contributes to the overall programme of study.

Learning Outcome 1

An understanding of the motivations for, and appropriate use of, agent-based computing.

This learning outcome is evidenced primarily through Collaborative Discussion 1 (Units 1-3), in which I explored the rise of agent-based systems and the benefits they offer to organisations. Drawing on real-world examples such as the SimMobility platform and Singapore's Smart Mobility 2030 initiative, I demonstrated an understanding of why agent-based approaches have emerged as a response to the limitations of centralised software architectures in complex, dynamic environments (Adnan et al., 2016; Krishnan, 2025). The group project further reinforced this outcome, requiring the team to justify the use of a BDI multi-agent architecture to address the specific operational needs of Insight Analytics Ltd.

Learning Outcome 2

An understanding of the main agent models in use today and their grounding in artificial intelligence research.

This outcome is addressed through both the group project and Collaborative Discussion 2 (Units 5-7). In the group project, the team designed and implemented a Belief-Desire-Intention (BDI) multi-agent system, demonstrating understanding of one of the most widely used deliberative agent architectures. In Discussion 2, I explored Knowledge Query and Manipulation Language (KQML) as a communication protocol for multi-agent systems, comparing it with method invocation in Python and Java, and drawing on contemporary research into emerging frameworks such as MCP and A2A (Wooldridge, 2009; Jeong, 2025).

Learning Outcome 3

The knowledge and skills required to develop, deploy and evaluate the tools and techniques of intelligent systems to solve real-world problems.

This outcome is most directly evidenced through the individual technical work completed as part of the project. I designed UML architecture diagrams, wrote and tested Python code for the BDI pipeline, and validated the system through a comprehensive test suite of 22 tests across 6 test suites. The parse tree activity (Unit 4) further demonstrates practical application of NLP concepts, a key area within intelligent systems research (Jurafsky and Martin, 2023). Collaborative Discussion 3 (Units 9-11) also contributes here, evaluating the ethical dimensions of deploying generative AI technologies such as DALL-E and ChatGPT in real-world contexts.

Learning Outcome 4

An understanding of contemporary research issues in the area of intelligent agent systems.

This outcome is evidenced across all three collaborative discussion forums and the reflective piece. Discussion 1 engaged with current research on the rise of agentic AI and autonomous decision-making systems. Discussion 2 addressed contemporary developments in agent communication, including emerging protocols such as MCP and Google's A2A framework. Discussion 3 examined the ethical implications of large language models and generative AI - one of the most pressing contemporary research areas in intelligent systems. The reflective piece also identifies deepfake regulation and the EU AI Act as areas of ongoing research interest, demonstrating awareness of the evolving sociotechnical landscape surrounding intelligent agent technologies.
